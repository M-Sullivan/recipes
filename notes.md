## Nutrition Basics

### Macronutrients

#### Proteins
- **Function**: Building and repairing tissues, enzyme production, immune function
- **Daily needs**: 0.8-1.0g per kg of body weight (higher for athletes)
- **Quality sources**:
  - Complete proteins: Meat, fish, poultry, eggs, dairy, quinoa, soy
  - Plant-based: Legumes, nuts, seeds, whole grains (combine for complete amino acids)
- **Cooking tips**: Avoid overcooking to preserve amino acids; grilling and baking preserve protein better than deep frying

#### Carbohydrates
- **Function**: Primary energy source, brain fuel, fiber for digestion
- **Types**:
  - **Simple carbs**: Quick energy but can spike blood sugar (fruits, honey, table sugar)
  - **Complex carbs**: Sustained energy, more nutrients (whole grains, legumes, vegetables)
- **Daily needs**: 45-65% of total calories (prioritize complex carbs)
- **Best sources**: 
  - Whole grains (brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole wheat)
  - Starchy vegetables (sweet potatoes, squash, pumpkin)
  - Legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas)
- **What to limit**: Refined grains, sugary drinks, processed snacks

#### Fats
- **Function**: Hormone production, vitamin absorption, brain health, energy storage
- **Daily needs**: 20-35% of total calories
- **Types**:
  - **Healthy fats (prioritize these)**:
    - Monounsaturated: Olive oil, avocados, nuts
    - Polyunsaturated (Omega-3 & Omega-6): Fatty fish, flaxseeds, walnuts, chia seeds
  - **Limit saturated fats**: Red meat, butter, cheese, coconut oil (use in moderation)
  - **Avoid trans fats**: Partially hydrogenated oils, most processed baked goods
- **Cooking applications**:
  - High-heat cooking: Avocado oil, refined olive oil
  - Medium-heat: Extra virgin olive oil, coconut oil
  - Low/no heat: Flaxseed oil, walnut oil (for dressings)

#### Fiber
- **Function**: Digestive health, blood sugar regulation, cholesterol management, satiety
- **Daily needs**: 25-30g for adults
- **Sources**: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, seeds
- **Tips**: Increase gradually and drink plenty of water

### Micronutrients

#### Essential Vitamins

**Fat-Soluble Vitamins** (A, D, E, K)
- **Vitamin A**: Vision, immune function, skin health
  - Sources: Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, liver, eggs
- **Vitamin D**: Bone health, immune function, mood
  - Sources: Fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified foods, sunlight
- **Vitamin E**: Antioxidant, skin health
  - Sources: Nuts, seeds, spinach, avocados
- **Vitamin K**: Blood clotting, bone health
  - Sources: Leafy greens, broccoli, Brussels sprouts

**Water-Soluble Vitamins** (B-complex, C)
- **B Vitamins**: Energy metabolism, brain function, red blood cell formation
  - B12 (animal products, fortified foods)
  - Folate (leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains)
  - B6 (poultry, fish, potatoes, bananas)
- **Vitamin C**: Immune function, collagen production, iron absorption
  - Sources: Citrus fruits, berries, bell peppers, broccoli
  - Cooking note: Heat-sensitive, eat raw or lightly cooked

#### Essential Minerals

- **Calcium**: Bone health, muscle function
  - Sources: Dairy, leafy greens, fortified foods, sardines
- **Iron**: Oxygen transport, energy
  - Heme iron (better absorbed): Red meat, poultry, fish
  - Non-heme iron: Legumes, spinach, fortified grains (pair with vitamin C)
- **Magnesium**: Muscle function, nerve function, bone health
  - Sources: Nuts, seeds, whole grains, leafy greens, dark chocolate
- **Potassium**: Blood pressure regulation, fluid balance
  - Sources: Bananas, potatoes, beans, spinach, avocados
- **Zinc**: Immune function, wound healing
  - Sources: Meat, shellfish, legumes, seeds, nuts
- **Selenium**: Antioxidant, thyroid function
  - Sources: Brazil nuts, seafood, meat, whole grains

### Hydration
- **Daily needs**: ~8-10 cups (2-2.5 liters), more with exercise/heat
- **Benefits**: Temperature regulation, nutrient transport, digestion, cognitive function
- **Tips**: 
  - Drink water throughout the day
  - Eat water-rich foods (cucumbers, watermelon, soups)
  - Limit caffeine and alcohol (diuretics)

## Cooking Techniques

### Dry-Heat Methods

#### Roasting
- **Temperature**: 350-450°F (175-230°C)
- **Best for**: Vegetables, poultry, larger cuts of meat
- **Health benefits**: Minimal added fat needed, caramelization enhances flavor
- **Tips**: 
  - Use a light coating of oil to prevent drying
  - Don't overcrowd the pan
  - Higher temps for crispy exteriors, lower for tender results

#### Baking
- **Temperature**: 300-375°F (150-190°C)
- **Best for**: Casseroles, fish, breads, lean meats
- **Health benefits**: No added fats required, even cooking
- **Tips**: Use parchment paper to reduce oil needs

#### Grilling
- **Best for**: Lean meats, fish, vegetables, fruits
- **Health benefits**: Fat drips away, adds smoky flavor without excess calories
- **Cautions**: 
  - Avoid charring (can create harmful compounds)
  - Marinate meats to reduce carcinogen formation
  - Use medium heat and flip frequently

#### Broiling
- **Best for**: Quick-cooking items, finishing dishes
- **Health benefits**: Similar to grilling, fast cooking preserves nutrients
- **Tips**: Watch carefully to prevent burning

#### Sautéing
- **Temperature**: Medium-high heat
- **Best for**: Vegetables, thinly sliced meats, seafood
- **Health benefits**: Quick cooking preserves nutrients, minimal oil needed
- **Tips**: 
  - Use 1-2 tablespoons of healthy oil
  - Keep food moving in the pan
  - Don't overcrowd

#### Stir-Frying
- **Temperature**: High heat
- **Best for**: Vegetables, bite-sized proteins, Asian dishes
- **Health benefits**: Very quick cooking, preserves nutrients and color
- **Tips**: 
  - Prep all ingredients first
  - Use minimal oil (1-2 tbsp)
  - Keep ingredients moving constantly

### Moist-Heat Methods

#### Steaming
- **Best for**: Vegetables, fish, dumplings
- **Health benefits**: No added fat, preserves water-soluble vitamins best
- **Tips**: 
  - Don't over-steam vegetables (keep them vibrant)
  - Season after cooking or use aromatic water
  - Layer flavors with herbs in steaming water

#### Boiling/Simmering
- **Boiling**: 212°F (100°C), vigorous bubbles
- **Simmering**: 180-205°F (82-96°C), gentle bubbles
- **Best for**: Pasta, grains, stocks, tougher cuts of meat
- **Nutrient considerations**: 
  - Some vitamins leach into water
  - Use cooking liquid in soups/sauces when possible
- **Tips**: Salt pasta water, don't over-boil vegetables

#### Poaching
- **Temperature**: 160-180°F (71-82°C)
- **Best for**: Eggs, fish, chicken breasts, fruits
- **Health benefits**: No added fat, gentle cooking, moist results
- **Tips**: 
  - Use flavorful liquids (broth, wine, citrus)
  - Maintain temperature below boiling
  - Perfect for delicate proteins

#### Braising
- **Method**: Sear, then cook slowly in liquid
- **Best for**: Tougher cuts of meat, root vegetables
- **Health benefits**: Tenderizes lean cuts, creates flavorful sauces
- **Tips**: 
  - Use minimal oil for searing
  - Include vegetables and herbs for flavor
  - Skim fat from surface before serving

#### Slow Cooking
- **Temperature**: Low (200°F) to High (300°F)
- **Best for**: Soups, stews, tough cuts, beans
- **Health benefits**: Hands-off, tenderizes lean proteins, infuses flavors
- **Tips**: 
  - Trim visible fat from meats
  - Layer vegetables at bottom
  - Add fresh herbs at the end

### Combination Methods

#### Pressure Cooking
- **Best for**: Beans, grains, tough meats, soups
- **Health benefits**: Fast cooking preserves nutrients, no added fat needed
- **Tips**: Follow liquid requirements, don't overfill

#### Air Frying
- **Best for**: Foods typically fried, vegetables, proteins
- **Health benefits**: Crispy results with 70-80% less fat than deep frying
- **Tips**: 
  - Don't overcrowd basket
  - Light oil spray enhances crispiness
  - Shake/flip halfway through

## Healthy Eating Principles

### The Plate Method
- **Half the plate**: Non-starchy vegetables (leafy greens, broccoli, peppers, tomatoes)
- **Quarter of the plate**: Lean protein (chicken, fish, tofu, legumes)
- **Quarter of the plate**: Complex carbohydrates (whole grains, starchy vegetables)
- **Add**: Small amount of healthy fat (avocado, nuts, olive oil)

### Whole Foods vs. Processed Foods

#### Whole Foods (Prioritize)
- **Definition**: Foods in their natural state or minimally processed
- **Examples**: Fresh fruits and vegetables, whole grains, nuts, seeds, lean meats, fish
- **Benefits**: 
  - Higher nutrient density
  - More fiber
  - No added sugars, sodium, or preservatives
  - Better satiety

#### Minimally Processed (Fine in moderation)
- **Examples**: Frozen vegetables, canned beans (low sodium), plain Greek yogurt, whole grain bread
- **Tips**: Check ingredients—fewer is better

#### Ultra-Processed (Limit)
- **Examples**: Chips, cookies, sugary cereals, frozen dinners, fast food
- **Why limit**: High in sodium, sugar, unhealthy fats; low in nutrients
- **Guideline**: Make these occasional treats, not daily staples

### Portion Control

#### Visual Cues
- **Protein**: Palm of your hand or deck of cards (3-4 oz)
- **Carbs**: Cupped hand or tennis ball (1/2 - 1 cup)
- **Fats**: Thumb tip or dice (1 tbsp oil, 1 oz nuts)
- **Vegetables**: Two cupped hands (unlimited non-starchy)

#### Practical Strategies
- Use smaller plates (8-10 inches)
- Measure portions initially to calibrate
- Pre-portion snacks
- Don't eat from packages
- Practice mindful eating (eat slowly, savor)

### Reducing Sodium
- **Recommended limit**: 2,300mg/day (1,500mg for high blood pressure)
- **Strategies**:
  - Cook from scratch
  - Use herbs and spices for flavor
  - Choose "no salt added" canned goods
  - Rinse canned beans and vegetables
  - Limit processed meats, cheese, condiments
  - Taste before adding salt

### Reducing Added Sugars
- **Recommended limit**: <10% of daily calories (~50g for 2,000 calories)
- **Hidden sources**: Sauces, dressings, yogurt, granola, bread
- **Strategies**:
  - Read labels (watch for multiple sugar names)
  - Choose unsweetened versions
  - Use fruit for sweetness
  - Gradually reduce sugar in recipes
  - Limit sugary drinks (biggest source)

### Increasing Fiber
- **Benefits**: Digestive health, stable blood sugar, cholesterol management, weight control
- **Strategies**:
  - Choose whole grains over refined
  - Eat the skin on fruits/vegetables when possible
  - Add beans/lentils to meals
  - Snack on fruits, vegetables, nuts
  - Include seeds (chia, flax, hemp)

### Plant-Based Eating
- **Flexitarian approach**: Mostly plants, some animal products
- **Benefits**: 
  - Higher in fiber, antioxidants, phytonutrients
  - Lower risk of chronic diseases
  - Environmental benefits
- **Key considerations**:
  - Ensure adequate protein from varied sources
  - B12 supplementation if fully plant-based
  - Combine plant proteins for complete amino acids
  - Include omega-3 sources (flax, chia, walnuts, algae)

### Mindful Eating
- **Practice**:
  - Eat without distractions (no TV, phone)
  - Chew thoroughly
  - Put utensils down between bites
  - Notice hunger and fullness cues
  - Savor flavors and textures
- **Benefits**: Better digestion, appropriate portions, more satisfaction

### The 80/20 Rule
- **Principle**: Eat nutritiously 80% of the time, enjoy treats 20%
- **Purpose**: Sustainability, avoiding restriction mindset
- **Application**: Focus on progress, not perfection

## Meal Planning & Preparation

### Weekly Meal Planning

#### Benefits
- Saves time and money
- Reduces food waste
- Ensures balanced nutrition
- Reduces decision fatigue
- Minimizes unhealthy impulse eating

#### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Inventory**: Check pantry, fridge, freezer
2. **Plan**: Choose recipes for the week
   - Include variety of proteins and vegetables
   - Consider your schedule (quick meals on busy days)
   - Plan leftovers strategically
3. **List**: Create organized grocery list by section
4. **Shop**: Stick to the list, shop perimeter first
5. **Prep**: Dedicate 1-2 hours to prep basics

### Meal Prep Strategies

#### Batch Cooking
- **What**: Cook large quantities of staples
- **Best for**: Grains, proteins, soups, sauces
- **Examples**:
  - Cook several pounds of chicken breasts
  - Make a big pot of quinoa or brown rice
  - Prepare 2-3 different soups
- **Storage**: Portion into containers, refrigerate (3-4 days) or freeze (2-3 months)

#### Ingredient Prep
- **Vegetables**: Wash, chop, store in containers
  - Salad greens: Wash, dry thoroughly, store with paper towel
  - Root vegetables: Chop, store in water
  - Peppers/onions: Chop, store in containers
- **Proteins**: Marinate, portion, or cook ahead
- **Grains**: Cook in bulk, refrigerate or freeze
- **Snacks**: Portion nuts, cut vegetables, make energy balls

#### Complete Meals
- **Method**: Assemble full meals in containers
- **Formula**: Protein + grain + vegetables + sauce
- **Examples**:
  - Chicken, rice, broccoli, teriyaki sauce
  - Ground turkey, quinoa, roasted vegetables, salsa
  - Tofu, noodles, stir-fry vegetables, peanut sauce

### Budget-Friendly Tips
- Buy seasonal produce
- Purchase frozen fruits and vegetables (equally nutritious)
- Buy in bulk (grains, beans, nuts)
- Choose cheaper protein sources (eggs, beans, chicken thighs)
- Reduce food waste (use scraps for stock, freeze extras)
- Plan meals around sales
- Cook from scratch
- Grow herbs at home

### Time-Saving Strategies
- Use a slow cooker or pressure cooker
- Sheet pan meals (everything roasts together)
- One-pot meals (less cleanup)
- Pre-cut vegetables (when budget allows)
- Double recipes and freeze half
- Keep convenience items on hand (rotisserie chicken, pre-cooked grains)
- Use versatile ingredients across multiple meals

## Ingredient Substitutions

### Lower Calorie Swaps

#### Dairy
- **Heavy cream** → Greek yogurt, coconut milk (lite), pureed cottage cheese
- **Sour cream** → Plain Greek yogurt, cottage cheese blended smooth
- **Cream cheese** → Neufchâtel cheese, Greek yogurt cream cheese
- **Butter** (in baking) → Applesauce, mashed banana, Greek yogurt (use 1/2 amount)
- **Whole milk** → Almond milk, oat milk, skim milk

#### Proteins
- **Ground beef** → Ground turkey, ground chicken, lentils, mushrooms (for part)
- **Bacon** → Turkey bacon, tempeh bacon, smoked paprika for flavor
- **Fried chicken** → Baked with whole wheat breadcrumb coating
- **Fatty cuts of meat** → Lean cuts (sirloin, tenderloin, chicken breast)

#### Carbohydrates
- **White rice** → Brown rice, quinoa, cauliflower rice, bulgur
- **White pasta** → Whole wheat pasta, chickpea pasta, zucchini noodles, spaghetti squash
- **White bread** → Whole grain bread, Ezekiel bread, lettuce wraps
- **Pizza crust** → Cauliflower crust, whole wheat crust, portobello mushroom caps
- **Tortillas** → Lettuce wraps, whole wheat tortillas, corn tortillas

#### Fats & Oils
- **Mayonnaise** → Greek yogurt, mashed avocado, hummus
- **Oil in baking** → Applesauce, mashed banana, pumpkin puree
- **Salad dressing** → Olive oil + vinegar, Greek yogurt-based dressing
- **Chips** → Air-popped popcorn, baked veggie chips, roasted chickpeas

#### Sweeteners
- **Sugar** (in baking) → Reduce by 1/4-1/3, use applesauce, mashed banana, dates
- **Syrup** → Fresh fruit, sugar-free syrup (sparingly), pureed fruit
- **Candy** → Dark chocolate (70%+), dried fruit, frozen grapes

### Allergen-Friendly Substitutions

#### Gluten-Free
- **All-purpose flour** → Gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, coconut flour, oat flour
- **Breadcrumbs** → Gluten-free breadcrumbs, crushed rice crackers, ground almonds
- **Soy sauce** → Tamari, coconut aminos

#### Dairy-Free
- **Milk** → Almond, oat, soy, coconut, cashew milk
- **Butter** → Coconut oil, vegan butter, olive oil
- **Cheese** → Nutritional yeast, cashew cheese, store-bought vegan cheese
- **Yogurt** → Coconut yogurt, almond yogurt, oat yogurt

#### Egg-Free
- **Eggs in baking** (per egg):
  - 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water (let sit 5 min)
  - 1 tbsp chia seeds + 3 tbsp water (let sit 5 min)
  - 1/4 cup applesauce
  - 1/4 cup mashed banana
  - 1/4 cup silken tofu (blended)
- **Eggs in binding** (meatballs, etc.): Breadcrumbs soaked in milk alternative

#### Nut-Free
- **Almond flour** → Sunflower seed flour, oat flour
- **Nut butter** → Sunflower seed butter, tahini
- **Almond milk** → Oat milk, rice milk

### Flavor Enhancers (Low-Calorie)

#### Instead of Salt
- Fresh or dried herbs (basil, oregano, thyme, rosemary)
- Spices (cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander)
- Citrus juice and zest
- Vinegars (balsamic, apple cider, rice)
- Garlic and onion (fresh or powder)
- Hot sauce or chili flakes
- Nutritional yeast

#### Instead of Sugar
- Cinnamon (enhances perceived sweetness)
- Vanilla extract
- Fresh or dried fruit
- Dates (pureed for baking)
- Small amounts of honey or maple syrup (natural but still sugar)

## Food Safety

### Safe Cooking Temperatures
- **Poultry** (whole): 165°F (74°C)
- **Ground meats**: 160°F (71°C)
- **Beef, pork, lamb** (whole cuts): 145°F (63°C) + 3 min rest
- **Fish**: 145°F (63°C)
- **Eggs**: Cook until yolk and white are firm
- **Leftovers**: Reheat to 165°F (74°C)

**Use a food thermometer**—color is not a reliable indicator.

### Storage Guidelines

#### Refrigerator (40°F/4°C or below)
- **Raw ground meat/poultry**: 1-2 days
- **Raw steaks, chops, roasts**: 3-5 days
- **Raw poultry**: 1-2 days
- **Raw fish**: 1-2 days
- **Cooked meat/poultry**: 3-4 days
- **Cooked fish**: 3-4 days
- **Eggs**: 3-5 weeks
- **Egg-based dishes**: 3-4 days
- **Soups/stews**: 3-4 days
- **Cooked vegetables**: 3-4 days

#### Freezer (0°F/-18°C or below)
- **Ground meat**: 3-4 months
- **Steaks, chops**: 4-12 months
- **Poultry**: 9-12 months (whole), 6-9 months (pieces)
- **Fish**: 6-8 months (fatty fish), 8-12 months (lean fish)
- **Cooked meals**: 2-3 months
- **Soups/stews**: 2-3 months
- **Bread**: 3 months

### Safe Food Handling

#### The Four Core Principles

**1. Clean**
- Wash hands for 20 seconds before/after handling food
- Wash produce under running water
- Clean cutting boards, utensils, and surfaces with hot soapy water
- Sanitize sponges regularly (microwave wet or dishwasher)

**2. Separate**
- Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and produce
- Keep raw meat, poultry, and seafood separated in fridge
- Marinate in refrigerator, never reuse marinade from raw meat
- Store raw meat on bottom shelf to prevent dripping

**3. Cook**
- Use a food thermometer to verify doneness
- Cook food thoroughly to kill harmful bacteria
- Don't partially cook meat/poultry then finish later

**4. Chill**
- Refrigerate perishables within 2 hours (1 hour if above 90°F)
- Don't overfill fridge (air needs to circulate)
- Thaw food in refrigerator, cold water, or microwave—never on counter
- Cool large batches quickly (divide into smaller containers, ice bath)

### Expiration Date Labels
- **"Sell by"**: For retailers, food is still safe after this date
- **"Best by/Best before"**: Quality date, not safety date
- **"Use by"**: Last date recommended for peak quality
- **When in doubt**: Look, smell, check texture—if questionable, throw it out

## Additional Resources & Tips

### Reading Nutrition Labels

#### Serving Size
- **Check this first**: All numbers are per serving
- **Compare to what you'll actually eat**: Package may contain multiple servings

#### Key Numbers to Check
1. **Calories**: Energy provided per serving
2. **Total fat**: Aim for mostly unsaturated
3. **Saturated fat**: Limit to <10% daily calories
4. **Trans fat**: Avoid (should be 0g)
5. **Sodium**: Compare products, choose lowest
6. **Total carbohydrates**: Includes fiber and sugars
7. **Fiber**: Higher is better (aim for 3g+ per serving)
8. **Added sugars**: Limit (check ingredients list for sources)
9. **Protein**: Adequate intake throughout the day

#### Ingredients List
- Listed by weight (most to least)
- Fewer ingredients usually better
- Watch for multiple sugar names (corn syrup, dextrose, maltose, etc.)
- Avoid partially hydrogenated oils (trans fats)

#### Percent Daily Value (%DV)
- **5% or less**: Low in that nutrient
- **20% or more**: High in that nutrient
- Aim high for: Fiber, vitamins, minerals
- Aim low for: Saturated fat, sodium, added sugars

### Special Dietary Considerations

#### Vegetarian/Vegan
- Ensure complete proteins (combine grains + legumes)
- Focus on B12 supplementation (vegans)
- Include iron-rich plant foods + vitamin C
- Adequate omega-3 (flax, chia, walnuts, algae supplements)
- Calcium from fortified foods or supplements

#### Gluten-Free
- Choose naturally gluten-free whole foods
- Be aware of cross-contamination
- Read labels carefully (hidden gluten)
- Ensure adequate fiber from GF whole grains

#### Low-Carb/Keto
- Prioritize healthy fats
- Include non-starchy vegetables
- Ensure adequate protein
- Stay hydrated and monitor electrolytes

#### Diabetic-Friendly
- Focus on complex carbs with fiber
- Pair carbs with protein/fat (slows absorption)
- Monitor portion sizes
- Spread carbs throughout the day
- Choose low glycemic index foods

## Final Thoughts

Healthy eating and cooking is a journey, not a destination. Focus on:
- **Progress over perfection**: Small, consistent changes add up
- **Variety**: Different foods provide different nutrients
- **Moderation**: All foods can fit in a balanced diet
- **Enjoyment**: Food should be pleasurable and nourishing
- **Sustainability**: Choose habits you can maintain long-term
- **Flexibility**: Adapt to your lifestyle, preferences, and budget

Remember: The best diet is one you can stick to that includes mostly whole foods, plenty of plants, adequate protein, and healthy fats—while still allowing room for foods you love.
