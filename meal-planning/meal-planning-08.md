Templates make meal planning faster and easier. Use these as starting points and customize to your needs.

## What Are Meal Planning Templates?

**Definition:** Pre-structured frameworks for planning your weekly meals.

**Benefits:**
- Reduces decision fatigue
- Speeds up planning process
- Ensures variety
- Maintains balance
- Easy to repeat

**How to Use:**
1. Choose a template that fits your lifestyle
2. Fill in specific meals/recipes
3. Adjust as needed
4. Repeat and refine over time

---

## Template 1: The Classic Weekly Planner

### Structure

**Basic 7-Day Grid:**
- 7 days (Monday-Sunday)
- 3 main meals (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner)
- Optional: Snacks column

### When to Use
- Traditional 3-meals-a-day eating
- Families
- Structured schedules
- Comprehensive planning

**Each Night Has a Theme:**
- Provides framework
- Variety within consistency
- Easy to remember
- Fun for families

**Option A: Classic Themes**
- Monday: Meatless Monday
- Tuesday: Taco Tuesday
- Wednesday: Whatever Wednesday (leftovers)
- Thursday: Throwback Thursday (comfort food)
- Friday: Fish Friday or Pizza Friday
- Saturday: Slow Cooker Saturday
- Sunday: Sunday Dinner (family meal)

- Monday: Mexican Monday
- Tuesday: Italian Tuesday
- Wednesday: Asian Wednesday
- Thursday: American Thursday
- Friday: Mediterranean Friday
- Saturday: Breakfast for Dinner Saturday

- Monday: Slow Cooker Monday
- Tuesday: Sheet Pan Tuesday
- Wednesday: One-Pot Wednesday

## Customizing Your Template

### Make It Work for YOU

**Add What You Need:**
- Meal prep task list
- Grocery budget tracker
- Nutrition notes
- Leftover tracker
- Calendar/schedule integration
- Notes section

**Remove What You Don't:**
- Don't need snacks column? Remove it
- Skip breakfast planning? Focus on dinner
- Weekend always flexible? Don't plan it

### Digital vs. Paper

**Digital Options:**
- Google Sheets/Excel (easy to copy week-to-week)
- Note-taking apps (Notion, Evernote)
- Meal planning apps (Plan to Eat, Mealime, Paprika)
- Pros: Easy to edit, copy, share
- Cons: Needs device, less tactile

**Paper Options:**
- Printable templates (laminate and use dry-erase)
- Notebooks or meal planning pads
- Whiteboard or chalkboard
- Pros: Tactile, visible, no screen
- Cons: Can't copy easily, less searchable

**Best Approach:** Use what you'll actually use consistently!

## Template Success Tips

### 1. Start Simple
- Don't overcomplicate
- Begin with basic template
- Add complexity as you get comfortable

### 2. Be Consistent (At First)
- Use same template for 4+ weeks
- Develop habits and rhythm
- Then adjust as needed

### 3. Keep It Visible
- Post on fridge
- Inside cabinet door
- Digital calendar
- Where you'll see it daily

### 4. Review Weekly
- What worked?
- What didn't?
- Adjust next week

### 5. Create a "Template Library"
- Save successful weeks
- Rotate through favorites
- Build personal collection

### 6. Share with Household
- Everyone knows the plan
- Can contribute ideas
- Buy-in from family

### 7. Be Flexible
- Template is GUIDE, not law
- OK to swap days
- OK to change plans
- Life happens!
